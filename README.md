## üí° The AI Foundation: Understanding Machine English

Generative AI systems do not genuinely understand human language; they **predict** the most likely next word or token based on probability and proximity.

1.  **AI Mechanics:** AI converts text into tokens (words or parts of words), which are converted into multi-dimensional vectors (numbers) and placed in a mathematical embedding space. Similar ideas live closer together in this space.
2.  **The Result:** Since the AI is a "guessing machine," vague prompts yield vague guesses. **Machine English** helps the AI **compute** your intent, not just try to comprehend it, by using sharp and targeted prompts.

#### **Debugging Cheat Codes (Step 4)**

Use these patterns when an output is weak or off, assuming the fault is with your prompt or context:

1.  **Chain of Thought Pattern:** "Think step by step show your reasoning then give me the final concise answer".
2.  **Verifier Pattern:** Ask the AI to clarify your intent: "ask me three questions that would clarify my intent to you... and then combine what you've learned and try again".
3.  **Refinement Pattern:** Ask the AI to help you write a better prompt: "before answering propose two sharper versions of my question ask which one I prefer".

#### **Verification Methods (Step 6)**

Since AI models are generative by design and may confidently make things up ("illusion"), verification is mandatory:

1.  **Assumptions:** Ask the AI to "list every assumption you made and rank them each by confidence".
2.  **Sources:** Ask it to "site two independent sources for each major claim... include title URL and a one-line quote".
3.  **Counter Evidence:** Push the AI to "find one credible source that disagrees with your answer explain the dependencies".
4.  **Auditing:** Ask the AI to "recompute every figure show your math or code," which often causes numbers to change when the model slows down.
5.  **Cross-Model Verification:** Run the same prompt in different models (e.g., ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude), or feed the claims of one model into another and ask it to critique or verify them.

#### **Framework 3: The OCEAN Framework (Adding Taste)**

Use the OCEAN framework to argue with, push back on, and sharpen the AI's answers:

| Acronym | Stands For | Instruction to the Model |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **O** | **Original** | Push for nonobvious ideas: "give me three angles no one else has thought about".
| **C** | **Concrete** | Ensure the response uses names, examples, and numbers: "back every claim with one real example".
| **E** | **Evident** | Make the reasoning visible: "show your logic in three bullets provide evidence before you provide final answer".
| **A** | **Assertive** | Demand that the response takes a stance and defends a thesis: "pick a side state your thesis defend it and then address the best counterpoint".
| **N** | **Narrative** | Guide the AI to write a flowing story using elements like hook, problem, insight, proof, and actions.
