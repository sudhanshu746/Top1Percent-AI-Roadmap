## Week 1: Learning Machine English and the AIM Framework

### Understanding How AI Works

1. **Tokenization:** AI breaks text into tokens (words or word parts)
2. **Vectorization:** Each token becomes a multi-dimensional vector (numbers)
3. **Embedding Space:** Vectors exist in a mathematical space where similar concepts cluster together
4. **Prediction:** AI predicts the next probable token based on context and proximity

### The AIM Framework (Step 1)

Structure your prompts using three components:

- **A - Actor:** Define who the AI is acting as (its persona)
- **I - Input:** Provide necessary context and data
- **M - Mission:** Specify exactly what you want the AI to do

**Example:**
```
Actor: "You are an expert marketing strategist with 15 years of experience."
Input: "Here is my product description: [your product details]"
Mission: "Review it and give me a bullet list of 10 specific ideas to improve conversion rates."
```

**Week 1 Goal:** Write structured AIM prompts without thinking.

## Week 2: Focus and Context

### Step 2: Pick Your Instrument

### Step 3: Context is the Map (MAP Framework)

Even the smartest AI sounds clueless without context. Use the MAP framework:

- **M - Memory:** Conversation history and notes from previous sessions
- **A - Assets:** Files, data, and resources you attach or paste
- **A - Actions:** Tools the model can use (web search, code execution, etc.)
- **P - Prompt:** Your instruction using the AIM framework

**Achievement Unlocked:** Using AIM + MAP places you in the **top 10% of AI users**.

## Week 3: Iteration and Verification

### Step 4: Debug Your Thinking

When answers are wrong, the problem is usually your prompt, not the AI. Use these debugging patterns:

#### Three Cheat Codes:

1. **Chain of Thought Pattern**
   ```
   "Think step by step, show your reasoning, then give me the final concise answer."
   ```

2. **Verifier Pattern**
   ```
   "Ask me three questions to clarify my intent, then combine what you've learned and try again."
   ```

3. **Refinement Pattern**
   ```
   "Propose two sharper versions of my question and ask which one I prefer."
   ```

### Step 5: Steer to Experts

Avoid generic, superficial answers by steering toward mastery:

- Navigate the model toward specific experts, frameworks, and depth
- Ask: "Who are the top experts on [topic]? What are the seminal papers and current thinking?"
- Feed that information back into the model for synthesis

### Step 6: Verification (Separate Intelligence from Illusion)

AI models are generative and will confidently hallucinate. Always verify using these five methods:

#### The Five Verification Methods:

1. **Assumptions**
   ```
   "List every assumption you made and rank them by confidence."
   ```

2. **Sources**
   ```
   "Cite two independent sources for each major claim, including title, URL, and a one-line quote."
   ```

3. **Counter Evidence**
   ```
   "Find a credible source that disagrees with your answer and explain the dependencies."
   ```

4. **Auditing**
   ```
   "Recompute every figure. Show your math or code."
   ```

5. **Cross-Model Verification**
   - Run the same prompt in different models (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude)
   - Take output from one model and ask another to critique it

**Week 3 Goal:** Feel in control of your AI output through iteration and verification.

## Week 4: Developing Taste

### Step 7: The OCEAN Framework

Move beyond generic "vending machine" output. Treat AI as a sparring partner to create tasteful insights that sound like you.

#### OCEAN Components:

- **O - Original**
  ```
  "Give me three angles no one else has thought about."
  ```

- **C - Concrete**
  - Demand names, examples, and numbers
  - Avoid abstract generalities

- **E - Evident**
  ```
  "Show your reasoning. Provide evidence before the final answer."
  ```

- **A - Assertive**
  ```
  "Take a stance, defend a thesis, and address the best counterpoint."
  ```

- **N - Narrative**
  - Hook: Grab attention
  - Problem: Define the challenge
  - Insight: Reveal the solution
  - Proof: Support with evidence
  - Actions: Provide next steps
